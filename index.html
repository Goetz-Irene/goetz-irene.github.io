Why Cats Are Special to Me

Cats have held a unique place in my heart ever since I was a child. Growing up after my parents’ divorce, I moved in with my grandmother, where my aunt also lived with her three cats. They became my first best friends and constant companions. One of them, a tolerant cat named Bacchus, shared an especially deep bond with me, despite a few humorous mishaps. Through my experiences with Bacchus and my own current pets, I’ve come to appreciate the distinct personalities and quirks that make each cat special.

My Three Cats

Sir Blueberry

Sir Blueberry, my first cat, is a four-year-old all-black cat with striking golden eyes. He was part of a litter I fostered, growing up alongside his five siblings until they were rehomed. Sir Blueberry has always been the “alpha” of the household, setting the tone for other pets. He's bold and insistent, never deterred by my requests to keep off the table while I’m eating. Even though he’s firm with the other animals, he’s incredibly gentle with my one-year-old son, displaying a calm patience that I deeply appreciate. Blueberry has an endearing habit of jumping onto my shoulders whenever I’m in the bathroom, which is both comforting and amusing.

Littl Smol Cat (Smalls)

Littl Smol Cat, or “Smalls” as he’s affectionately known, joined our family a few months after Blueberry. While “Smalls” started as a nickname, it eventually became his real name. Though timid around Blueberry, Smalls has his own form of loyalty. He spends most of the day hiding and only comes out at night to join me on the bed. This quiet nightly ritual is something I’ve come to cherish, as it’s a special time for just the two of us once the house has quieted down. His need for this ritual shows how much comfort he finds in my presence, even if he’s a bit reserved around the other pets.

Mavis

Mavis is our most recent addition—a cat who showed up in our garage and yard and eventually wandered inside, deciding to stay. Although Sir Blueberry and Smalls were initially wary, I’ve noticed signs of acceptance recently. A few times, I’ve woken up to find all three cats peacefully sleeping on my bed, a small but significant sign that they’re learning to tolerate one another. Mavis is still adjusting to her new surroundings, and I’m trying to ensure she feels welcomed and loved in her new home.

My Childhood with Bacchus

One of my earliest memories with cats is with Bacchus, one of my aunt’s cats who became my close friend when I was just a toddler. There’s a story my mother loves to tell about a time I tried to pick Bacchus up in a way he didn’t like, which led to him scratching me and digging his claws into my scalp. Even though it sounds dramatic, it was just a part of the learning experience. Despite this incident, Bacchus was always incredibly tolerant of me. He would still climb up and curl up on me whenever I lay on the couch or drank a bottle. Looking back, Bacchus taught me a lot about respecting boundaries while still enjoying companionship.

Life with Sol, Our Puppy

In addition to my three cats, I also have a seven-month-old German Shepherd mix named Sol. He’s a bundle of energy, weighing in at 42 pounds and constantly requiring supervision and training to keep the house intact. Sir Blueberry, despite being significantly smaller, has managed to establish himself as the “boss” over Sol, even intimidating him at times. This dynamic highlights Blueberry’s confident and assertive personality, and I love watching how he interacts with Sol, who respects his space despite his size advantage. It’s been a learning experience managing the differences between raising a high-maintenance puppy and living with more independent cats.

Why Cats Are Special

There’s something deeply comforting about cats. Unlike dogs, cats have an instinct to use a litter box, eat as needed, and entertain themselves. They’re low-maintenance, independent, and self-sufficient—traits that I find especially appealing. My puppy, Sol, requires constant attention and training, but the cats are more content to find their own rhythms and routines. Each of my cats has their own unique personality, from Blueberry’s boldness and leadership to Smalls’ quiet loyalty and Mavis’ curiosity. They bring a sense of calm and companionship that balances the hustle and bustle of life with a young family and a puppy.

I’ve come to realize that cats are truly remarkable companions. They might seem aloof at times, but they often show their affection in subtle ways. Whether it’s Sir Blueberry’s shoulder-jumping ritual or Smalls’ quiet nighttime visits, each moment reminds me of how special they are. Cats teach us about patience, independence, and the beauty of quiet companionship, making them truly one-of-a-kind.

Fun Facts About Cats

  • Cats spend about 70% of their lives sleeping.
  • A cat's nose print is unique, similar to a human fingerprint.
  • Domestic cats share 95.6% of their DNA with tigers.
  • Cats can make about 100 different sounds, a wide range compared to dogs.
  • Cats usually have five toes on their front paws and four on their back paws.
